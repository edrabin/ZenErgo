Three Decades of Clinical Insight, Now in Your Office

Dr. Ed Rabin has spent over thirty years as a practicing chiropractor, working with patients across the full spectrum of musculoskeletal conditions — from acute injuries to chronic, work-related disorders that develop slowly and silently over years.

Throughout that career, a pattern became impossible to ignore: a significant portion of the patients who walked through the door were suffering not from accidents or isolated incidents, but from the cumulative effects of how they worked every day. Poor workstation setup. Awkward monitor heights. Chairs that offered no real support. Keyboards positioned to strain rather than support. Habits that, individually, seemed harmless — but over thousands of hours, caused real damage.

"Ergonomics is not a luxury or a corporate wellness checkbox. It's clinical prevention — and for many people, it's the most important health intervention they'll never know they needed."

ZenErgo Solutions was founded to bring that clinical perspective directly to the workplace. Rather than waiting for injuries to progress to the point of requiring treatment, Dr. Rabin now works with individuals and organizations to assess and optimize the environments in which people spend the majority of their waking hours.

Why a Chiropractor?

Ergonomic assessors come from many backgrounds — occupational health, physical therapy, industrial engineering. Dr. Rabin's chiropractic background offers something distinct: Three decades of hands-on understanding of how the spine, joints, and soft tissues respond to sustained mechanical stress, combined with the diagnostic fluency to identify early-stage musculoskeletal dysfunction and connect it directly to workstation factors.

That clinical depth is what separates a ZenErgo Solutions assessment from a generic workstation checklist. Every recommendation is grounded in an understanding of how the human body actually works — and how it fails when asked to work in ways it wasn't designed for.

Philosophy
How Dr. Rabin Approaches Every Assessment
  • 1
    Prevention over treatment

    The best outcome is an injury that never happens. Every assessment is oriented toward identifying and eliminating risk before it becomes a problem.

  • 2
    Clinical rigor, practical output

    Assessments are grounded in clinical science, but the deliverable — a comprehensive report — is designed to be clear, actionable, and immediately useful.

  • 3
    The whole person, not just the workstation

    A chair can be adjusted, but habits, movement patterns, and individual anatomy matter just as much. Every recommendation accounts for both.
